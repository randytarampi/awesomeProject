Acknowledgements

This might look like an official SFU website, but it's not. The general look and feel was borrowed from a variety of SFU webpages in order to maintain the "common look and feel" as perscribed by the SFU Branding Requirements. The banner above is from here.

Also, this project makes use of the HTML5 Boilerplate CSS reset.

Anyways, with that all said, thanks to the university for hopefully not suing us. Thanks also to our colleagues, friends and family, especially Kevin, Derek and Allison for fielding our questions, showing us what we should be doing, and (most importantly) feeding us.

Also thanks to our CMPT 470 instructor for not calling us crazy, even though we weren't sure if this was possible at the time. And thanks to Greg Baker for clueing us in as to where to find up-to-date semester data.

Dependencies

While the actual project you see is pretty small and self-contained, we were only able to get it as such by bringing in extra software.

That said, we've built this on top of Django 1.3.1. We're making use of Dajax & Dajaxice as well as jQuery 1.7.2 and jquery.ba-serializeobject. The developers made use of the django-debug-toolbar.
